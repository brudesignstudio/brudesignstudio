First impressions are made online now

By Bru Design Studio·3 min read

You have 0.05 seconds

Research from Google found that users form an opinion about a website in 50 milliseconds. That is faster than a blink. In that fraction of a second, a potential customer has already decided whether your business looks trustworthy, professional, and worth their time.

This is not a design opinion. It is a measurable behavior pattern that directly affects whether someone stays on your site and contacts you, or clicks away to your competitor.

Your website is your storefront

For most businesses today, the website is the first physical representation of the brand that a customer encounters. Before they see your office, your product, or your team, they see your website. That page is your storefront, your reception desk, and your first handshake all at once.

A storefront that is dark, cluttered, or hard to navigate turns people away before they ever give you a chance. The same is true online. A website that loads slowly, looks outdated, or is difficult to use on a mobile phone communicates to visitors that the experience of working with you might be similarly frustrating.

On the other hand, a clean, fast, and clearly structured website communicates competence, attention to detail, and professionalism. It tells the visitor that you run a tight operation and that their experience as a customer will reflect the same standard.

Mobile is not optional

More than 60 percent of web traffic now comes from mobile devices. If your website is not designed to perform well on a phone, you are delivering a broken first impression to the majority of people who visit you.

Google also uses mobile performance as a ranking factor, which means a poor mobile experience does not just frustrate users. It actively pushes you lower in search results, reducing the number of people who ever find you in the first place.

A professional website built with mobile-first principles ensures that your first impression is consistent and strong regardless of what device someone is using to find you.

What happens when there is no website

When a potential customer searches for your business and finds nothing, they do not assume you are a hidden gem. They assume you are out of business, too small to be credible, or simply not an option worth pursuing. The absence of a website is itself a negative signal.

In markets where your competitors do have a website, the contrast is immediate. Your competitor gets the benefit of a professional first impression. You get a search result that leads nowhere. The customer makes their choice in seconds.

Design is credibility

Bad design is not neutral. Studies on web credibility consistently show that poor visual design is the number one reason people distrust a website. Before they read a single word of your copy or look at a single product, they have reacted to the design.

This is why the investment in a properly designed website is not a vanity expense. It is a credibility investment. Every element from the typography to the color palette to the spacing communicates something about your brand, whether you intend it to or not.

When that design is intentional, consistent, and professional, it builds the trust that converts browsers into buyers.

Your first impression starts online. Make it count.
